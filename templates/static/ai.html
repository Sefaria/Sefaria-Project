Sefaria and AI: New Pathways Into Primary Texts

Sefaria was founded to maximize Torah’s impact in the digital age by making the Jewish library free, open, and interactive. Since our inception, we have leveraged the best available technologies to expand access to and deepen engagement with Jewish texts. Our founders’ foresight in making Jewish texts machine-readable continues to enable Sefaria, and others, to harness technological advancements such as artificial intelligence (AI), particularly large language models (LLMs).

Today, AI offers a powerful new set of tools that can accelerate research, support developers, and expand access. At the same time, generative AI, which is one branch of AI, can produce confident-sounding inaccuracies or overlook nuance, context, and the layered meanings embedded in Jewish texts. Our approach is grounded in a clear commitment to use AI to guide people to primary sources and deepen their understanding of them.

Pirkei Avot 1:1
Moses received the Torah at Sinai and transmitted it to Joshua, Joshua to the elders, and the elders to the prophets, and the prophets to the Men of the Great Assembly. They said three things: Be deliberate in justice, establish many students, and make a fence around the Torah.

משֶׁה קִבֵּל תּוֹרָה מִסִּינַי, וּמְסָרָהּ לִיהוֹשֻׁעַ, וִיהוֹשֻׁעַ לִזְקֵנִים, וּזְקֵנִים לִנְבִיאִים, וּנְבִיאִים מְסָרוּהָ לְאַנְשֵׁי כְנֶסֶת הַגְּדוֹלָה. הֵם אָמְרוּ שְׁלשָׁה דְבָרִים, הֱווּ מְתוּנִים בַּדִּין, וְהַעֲמִידוּ תַלְמִידִים הַרְבֵּה, וַעֲשׂוּ סְיָג לַתּוֹרָה:

Sefaria is part of a long and storied history of transmitting Jewish tradition, holding sacred the texts in our library. As we experiment with and introduce AI-generated content into the Sefaria Library, we are committing to specific guardrails that effectively serve as a fence around the Torah. For Sefaria’s use of AI, we have prioritized accuracy, transparency, and empowering learners to explore and interpret texts on their own.

  1. Transparency, always: We will clearly label AI-powered content and experiences, and be transparent about where, when, why, and how AI is used on Sefaria. We currently use two icons to designate AI-generated content:
    • The outlined star indicates AI-assisted content that has undergone human review.
    • The filled-in star indicates fully AI-generated content without human review.
  2. Citations-first approach: Sefaria’s AI experiences, like the Library Assistant, are built to keep learners anchored in the texts. AI content will always cite and direct learners to sources within the Library.
  3. Supported Learning: Sefaria’s use of AI is intended to be a learning support, not a replacement for rabbis, educators, or other trusted guides. In practice, that means we will not use AI to provide halakhic (Jewish legal) rulings or personal advice.
  4. Multivocality without editorializing: Many Jewish texts and commentaries provide differing perspectives and can even disagree. Sefaria’s approach to AI is designed to preserve that tradition, helping to surface multiple approaches. It is not meant to determine a single “right” answer, declare a definitive conclusion, or teach a single perspective.
  5. Feedback and continual evaluation: We will continually monitor and improve our AI features, actively seek user feedback, and make it easy to flag issues so our team can review and refine system behaviors.

Our AI Approach

As new tools have emerged and evolved, the Jewish world has always wrestled with how to use them thoughtfully. The history of Jewish texts is a testament to the constant evolution of technology, all of which have played their part in making learning possible — from manuscripts to printing presses to digital libraries. Today, Sefaria is engaging AI with the same seriousness, taking a design-driven approach: testing, learning, and refining. We carefully experiment, gather user feedback, and build only what supports learning, while preserving the integrity and sanctity of the texts. This commitment translates into three clear priorities:

  • Lower barries to entry: Support learners of all backgrounds in finding relevant primary sources in a language they understand, even if they don’t know what book to open or what keywords to search.
  • Protect the integrity of the tradition: As people increasingly ask AI systems for Torah, Sefaria wants the underlying citations, context, and textual provenance to remain reliable and checkable — title to verse.
  • Honor the full range of voices in our tradition: Jewish texts preserve debate across centuries. Our goal is to help users encounter that richness by surfacing multiple perspectives from across the library in response to contemporary questions.

AI Uses on Sefaria

LLM-Powered Linking

With the help of LLMs, Sefaria automatically identifies textual citations and links them to original sources in the Library in two ways:

  1. Interlinked texts within the Library: When reading texts on Sefaria, users can explore millions of intertextual links as clickable citations or as related texts in the Resource Panel.
  2. The Sefaria Linker: With a free JavaScript plugin, external websites can automatically make textual references link back to the original source on Sefaria. The Sefaria Linker is used by hundreds of websites and blogs, including resources like My Jewish Learning, Times of Israel, and the Orthodox Union.

Topics on Sefaria

Topics on Sefaria aggregates sources from Jewish texts thematically, helping users discover sources and gain context on key ideas. Of our 5,000+ Topic pages, 1,000 feature an enhanced format that includes AI-assisted source curation, headings, and introductions. AI-enhanced pages follow a Sefaria-created editorial framework and have been reviewed by our team.

Pirkei Avot Learning Guide

The Pirkei Avot Learning Guide is a supplemental tool for exploring Pirkei Avot, a third-century compilation of rabbinic teachings and ethical maxims. An interactive interface allows users to view pre-written questions and answers about the text, summarized from classic commentaries on Pirkei Avot by an AI model and then reviewed by the Sefaria team.

Relevance-Based Sheet Sorting

In the Sefaria Library, users can use the Resource Panel to locate user-created Sheets from Voices on Sefaria that relate to a selected passage of text. When viewing Sheets related to a specific passage from the Library, users then have the option to sort Sheets by relevance — a feature that utilizes large language models (LLMs) to assess the content's relevance to the passage at hand. Alternatively, results can be sorted in two non-AI powered ways: by number of views or by publication date.

AI-Assisted Translation

Since 2023, our team of text specialists has experimented with translation using various AI models, assessing for accuracy and fidelity to the original text. In 2026, we released our first AI-assisted translation: an English translation of the Kli Yakar Torah commentary, which was originally translated by Claude 3.7 and then underwent a comprehensive, six-month editing process led by Sefaria Translation and Research Specialist Francis Nataf.

Library Assistant

Sefaria’s Library Assistant is an experimental AI-powered chatbot for discovering and exploring texts in the Sefaria Library. Built upon Claude technology, it uses Sefaria data to provide cited, linked responses to Torah-based questions — with the built-in guardrails and sensitivities that our sacred textual tradition deserve.

AI Research & Development

Sefaria’s relationship with artificial intelligence (AI) began long before today’s chatbots. From the start, our founders made a core bet: build a free, open, machine-readable Jewish library so that Jewish texts can be searched, linked, reused, and studied at digital scale. Establishing that foundation meant using early AI tools, including optical character recognition (OCR) to digitize texts, followed by careful human review — an approach that reflects Sefaria’s emphasis on quality, trust, and usability.

As the Library grew, we used AI to strengthen one of Sefaria’s most important experiences: helping learners see how Jewish texts connect to one another. Large-language model (LLM) technology allowed us to continually increase interlinking between related texts, and for external websites to automatically create clickable citations when referencing Jewish sources in the Sefaria Library. In 2020, we launched Topics, a major LLM-enabled update that made it easier for users to discover sources by theme. In recent years, we have also added clearly labeled, editor-reviewed AI-written material on some Topic pages — always with a “learning first” approach so that AI supports study, rather than replacing teachers or trusted guidance.

In 2023, we began testing AI-assisted translation, with expert human review, so that more of the Library can be available in English. That same year, we started exploring what an AI-powered Sefaria-style learning assistant could look like After a multi-year testing phase, we built the Sefaria Library Assistant in 2025, and launched it in Spring 2026. As these tools evolve, we’ll keep researching carefully and using AI in ways that deepen access, strengthen trust, and invite more people into the ongoing conversation of Torah.
