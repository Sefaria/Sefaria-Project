Sefaria Summer Internships (Engineering)

Sefaria is looking for driven individuals with an interest in entrepreneurship to join our engineering team. Our 10-week paid summer program will expose interns to the inner workings of a fast-growing nonprofit startup.

Interns will play an integral role in creating mobile and/or web product for Sefaria. Sefaria’s CTO Brett Lockspeiser (based in San Francisco) will help define one or two projects for each intern to develop and manage from start to finish. Interns can expect full support and coaching from the Sefaria team to help meet project goals and grow as engineers. Other members of Sefaria’s engineering team live in New York and Jerusalem.

Requirements

  • Currently enrolled in a degree program or 2015 graduate
  • Background in computer science or engineering
  • Interest in Jewish learning
  • Solid written and verbal communication skills
  • Strong judgment and decision-making skills

Technologies

  • JavaScript, HTML, CSS
  • Python, Django
  • MongoDB
  • ElasticSearch
  • Android, iOS
  • D3.js

Location

  • Sefaria provides office space for interns in San Francisco, New York and Jerusalem. Interns are also welcome to work remotely. Sefaria cannot cover travel or living expenses.

Stipend

  • $3,500

To apply

  • Please send a brief cover letter and your resume to jobs@sefaria.org

About Sefaria: Sefaria is building the open, online, living library of Jewish texts that will establish the digital infrastructure for the future of Jewish study, education, and knowledge. Best-selling author Joshua Foer (“Moonwalking with Einstein”) and Brett Lockspeiser, leader of the Google team that developed the Google News Archive, co-founded Sefaria in 2013.

Sefaria is committed to making Jewish literature accessible and evolvable. We believe that Judaism’s remarkable textual tradition should be as easy to access as everything else online. Sefaria wants to make Jewish texts easy to find — both in the original format and in translation — and free, presented with a user interface that is elegant, intuitive and interactive. Beyond access, having a database of Jewish texts in a structured, digital format opens up new possibilities for creativity and study. For example, check out our interactive visualization of textual connections.
